For people who are thinking about buying this keep in mind any glare from extra lights like this in the interior can restrict your vision outside. In Ontario in can also be considered illegal according to the highway traffic act.

Legality & Safety: There is no specific regulation forbidding driving with interior lights on; however, they can create a dangerous, distracting glare at night, compromising safety.
Coloured Lights: Under O. Reg. 484/07,, interior lights that cast red or blue light outside the vehicle are prohibited for non-emergency vehicles. Only white or amber light is generally acceptable.
Visibility Requirements: Section 74(1) of the HTA requires that windshields and windows remain clear for a full view, meaning bright interior lights that create reflections and reduce visibility could lead to charges.
